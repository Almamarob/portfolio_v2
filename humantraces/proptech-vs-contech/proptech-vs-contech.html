PropTech vs ConTech

Two ecosystems, different languages: mapping the players, tools, and blind spots.

PropTech: The Real Estate Technology Stack

PropTech emerged to digitize and optimize the operational and financial layers of real estate. It focuses on what happens after construction: tenant management, building operations, portfolio analytics, transactions, and capital flows.

Core Categories

1. Asset & Property Management
Tools for managing buildings and tenant relationships. Examples: Yardi, MRI Software, Procore (operations side), HqO, Equiem (tenant experience platforms).

2. Smart Buildings & IoT
Sensor networks, building automation, energy management. Examples: Johnson Controls, Schneider Electric, Willow (digital twins), Mapped.

3. Portfolio & Investment Analytics
Financial modeling, risk assessment, ESG reporting, portfolio optimization. Examples: Altus Group, CoStar, REFM, Deepki (ESG), Measurabl.

4. Leasing & Transactions
CRE marketplaces, deal management, lease administration. Examples: VTS, Buildium, Reonomy, CompStak.

5. Facilities & Maintenance
Work order management, preventive maintenance, vendor coordination. Examples: ServiceChannel, Facilio, UpKeep.

Who Buys PropTech

Asset managers, landlords, REITs, institutional investors, facility managers. Budgets come from operational expense lines or investment committees. Decision cycles are long, but contracts are sticky.

PropTech's Blind Spot

PropTech inherits buildings as black boxes. It knows how a building performs now, but has no insight into how it was made. Construction quality, contractor reliability, as-built accuracy — all of that context is lost in the handoff.

ConTech: The Construction Technology Stack

ConTech focuses on the design and delivery phase — everything that happens before a building becomes operational. It's about project execution: planning, coordination, labor, materials, and risk mitigation.

Core Categories

1. Design & BIM (Building Information Modeling)
3D modeling, clash detection, design coordination. Examples: Autodesk (Revit, BIM 360), Trimble (SketchUp, Tekla), Graphisoft (ArchiCAD).

2. Project Management & Collaboration
Document control, RFIs, submittals, scheduling. Examples: Procore, PlanGrid (now part of Autodesk), Fieldwire, Buildertrend.

3. Site Logistics & Progress Tracking
Drones, computer vision, reality capture, progress monitoring. Examples: DroneDeploy, OpenSpace, Buildots, Disperse.

4. Workforce & Labor Management
Time tracking, safety compliance, crew coordination. Examples: Procore (labor module), Rhumbix, SmartBarrel, TradeTapp.

5. Procurement & Supply Chain
Material ordering, supplier management, cost tracking. Examples: BuildingConnected (Oracle), Kojo, Corecon, Materialogic.

6. Risk, Safety & Compliance
Safety incident tracking, quality control, compliance documentation. Examples: Procore (safety module), iAuditor, SafetyCulture, SiteAware.

Who Buys ConTech

General contractors, subcontractors, construction managers, architects, engineers. Budgets are project-specific or part of overhead. ROI is measured in time saved, errors avoided, and cost overruns prevented.

ConTech's Blind Spot

ConTech tools are built for delivery, not legacy. Once a project wraps, the data gets archived or thrown away. There's no incentive to structure it for the asset manager who takes over, or the investor evaluating the portfolio.

The Overlaps (Where Things Get Interesting)

A few companies straddle both worlds:

  • Procore: Started as ConTech (project management), now expanding into PropTech (asset operations, owner tools).
  • Autodesk: Dominates ConTech (BIM, design tools), but increasingly pushing into PropTech (Tandem for digital twins, FM integrations).
  • Digital Twin Platforms: Willow, Matterport, RESO — trying to bridge construction and operations by creating persistent 3D models that live beyond the build phase.
  • ESG & Sustainability Tools: Deepki, Measurabl, Giatec (concrete sensing) — touching both construction (material choices, carbon tracking) and operations (energy performance, emissions reporting).

The Fundamental Difference

Dimension PropTech ConTech
Time Horizon Long-term (10-30 years) Short-term (12-36 months)
Primary User Asset managers, landlords, investors Contractors, architects, engineers
Value Metric NOI, occupancy, energy efficiency, ESG scores On-time delivery, budget adherence, safety records
Data Focus Operational metrics, financial performance Progress tracking, risk mitigation, execution details
Integration Priority Financial systems, lease management, CRE platforms BIM tools, scheduling software, procurement systems

Why They Don't Talk to Each Other

It's not just different software. It's different companies, different incentives, different departments, and different moments in a building's life. The handoff between ConTech and PropTech is a gap where data goes to die.

Until someone builds the bridge, the silos remain.
