Why Cities Matter

Real estate doesn't happen in the abstract. It happens in specific places, with specific rules, specific players, and specific constraints. Every market has its own rhythm, its own politics, its own unwritten rules about who builds what and where.

The framework matters less than the context. PropTech and ConTech tools don't operate in a vacuum. They succeed or fail based on local dynamics: regulation, ownership structures, labor markets, capital flows, cultural expectations.

This section maps the cities: each one represents a different lens on how real estate, construction, and technology intersect.

The goal it's to map the patterns that repeat across borders and the exceptions that define local markets. Real estate is global in its capital flows but ruthlessly local in its execution. This section is about understanding both.
